I took my first set of data points on 8/8/2024, a Thursday. Note that this article is being written AFTER the data was collected...

In this article we will cover:

  1. A quick explainer of the routes taken in collecting data.

  4. A display of the data and what went wrong in the data collection.
  5. Some statistical analysis (though we won't do too much of this as the data is flawed anyway).

I arrived at San Francisco 4th and King station around 8:00 AM and after having visited an office that a high school friend was interning at, I proceeded to take data around 8:25 AM...

I measured not only the total time between starting point and ending point, but time between any significant events within the trip as well...

For northbound runs (T Third Street), these times between significant events were:

  1. Walk time between Caltrain station to the T platform at 4th and King.
  2. Wait time on the 4th and King platform for a T train to arrive.
  3. Ride time between 4th and King to Union Square station on the T.
    • (I started measuring a rough time taken for the train to actually reach the Central Subway tunnel starting on my second run as well.)
  4. Walk time between Union Square station platform to the Dewey Monument.

For southbound runs (30 Stockton / 45 Union Stockton), these times between significant events were:

  1. Walk time from the Dewey Monument to the bus stop at Stockton and Geary.
  2. Wait time at the Stockton and Geary bus stop for the next 30 or 45 to arrive.
  3. Ride time between Stockton and Geary to Townsend and 4th near the Caltrain station.
  4. Walk time between Townsend and 4th to the Caltrain station.

An overview of the Northbound run involves:

  1. Leaving the Caltrain Station at 4th and King streets...
  2. Walking to the T platform...
  3. Waiting for the T Third Street train...
  4. Taking the T to Union Square station...
  5. Leaving Union Square station, and heading to the Dewey Monument...
